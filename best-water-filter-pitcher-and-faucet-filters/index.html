Best water filters for 2026

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

For most households, the Brita 10-Cup Water Filter Pitcher is the best overall pick – it strikes a reliable balance of cost, taste, and convenience, backed by decades of user trust.

The PUR Ultimate Faucet Mount is your go‑to if you want filtered water on tap without a pitcher taking up fridge space; the ZeroWater pitcher is unmatched for removing total dissolved solids; the Aquagear offers premium materials and a long filter life; and the Culligan FM‑15 is a solid budget faucet filter that gets the basics right.

Tap water quality varies wildly by location – from harmless minerals that affect taste to trace contaminants like lead or chlorine byproducts. A countertop water filter is one of the simplest, most affordable ways to improve what comes out of your faucet, whether you want better‑tasting drinking water, reduced sediment, or certified reduction of specific pollutants. When choosing, consider three things: what you want to remove (check certifications), how much filtered water you need daily (pitcher capacity versus faucet flow rate), and your space and budget. Pitchers are portable and don’t require installation, but they need refilling and fridge space. Faucet filters mount directly on the tap, giving you on‑demand filtered water without a separate container, though they can interfere with some pull‑down spray heads and may require an adapter.
